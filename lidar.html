Introduction

What is LiDAR?

Lidar, which stands for Light Detection and Ranging, is a remote sensing technology that uses laser light to measure distances and generate precise, three-dimensional information about the shape and characteristics of objects in its field of view. Lidar systems typically consist of a laser scanner, a GPS receiver, and an inertial navigation system.

How does it work?

LiDAR works by emitting laser pulses towards a target area and measuring the time it takes for the reflected light to return. By calculating the round-trip travel time and knowing the speed of light, LiDAR systems can accurately determine the distance to objects.

Implementation

  1. Shoot a projectile (laser) with velocity v and destroy it upon impact
  2. Calculate time on impact t
  3. Calculate the distance with the fomula d=v*t*a where a is a constant for transforming Unity units into the desired measuring system. This constant will differ based on the scale of the world.
Note: For best accuracy, make sure you set the projectile's collision detection to continuous.
